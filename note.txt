❯ so if client ask how to improve for both gan and tcn what to ans?

How to Improve the GAN

1. More stations
The biggest limitation. With only 3 stations, the GAN has almost no spatial signal. Add 5–10 nearby Pahang stations and imputation quality jumps significantly — if one is missing, 9 others can inform it.

2. Use a VAE instead of GAN
Variational Autoencoders are more stable for small tabular datasets. GANs are notoriously hard to train and often don't converge well with limited data. A VAE would likely give better R² on this dataset size.

3. More training data
495 missing values out of 18,000 is only 2.7%. The GAN doesn't have enough "practice" filling gaps. Longer historical records (pre-2009) would help.

---
How to Improve the TCN

1. Add meteorological inputs ← biggest impact by far
Right now the model only sees past rainfall. Add humidity, temperature, wind speed, air pressure — even from a nearby weather station. Daily R² could realistically jump from 0.07 to 0.3–0.5.

2. Add ENSO/climate indices
El Niño and La Niña directly control Malaysian monsoon intensity. Adding the monthly ONI (Oceanic Niño Index) as a feature is free data and highly predictive of seasonal patterns.

3. Longer historical record
4,190 training sequences is small for deep learning. 30+ years of data would substantially improve generalisation.

4. Transformer instead of TCN
Self-attention (like a weather Transformer) can learn longer-range dependencies than dilated convolutions. State-of-the-art rainfall papers in 2023–2024 use Transformer-based architectures.

5. Ensemble the predictions
Train 5–10 TCN models with different random seeds and average their outputs. Cheap to do, typically improves R² by 0.02–0.05 with no architecture changes.
